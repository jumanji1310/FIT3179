How does Australian internet compare to the rest of the world?

This visualisation compares various aspects of Australian internet to the rest of the world and analyses Australian internet itself. The data is based on sources from 2018 to 2022 including Ookla, Opensignal, Freedom on the Net and Wikipedia hence some visualisation are not fully up to date and conclusions are based on combining various sources.

Internet users percentage by country from 2020 to 2022

The map above shows the percentage of a country's population that has accessed internet with any device, including mobile phones. Australia is ranked 52nd in the world in terms of percentage of internet users at 81.60% with the highest being Monaco at 102.40% (possible due to internet users and population being estimates) and lowest at 1.7% by Somalia.

Average broadband and mobile speed from 2020 to 2021

Broadband price by country in 2022

The scatterplot displays the average broadband and mobile download speed in Megabits per second with broadband speed by Ookla in 2021 and mobile speed by Opensignal in 2020. The highest mobile and broadband speed for each continent is shown when filtered.

Australia has the highest average mobile speed at 100Mbits/s and ranked 41st in the world for broadband speed with 77.88Mbits/s. The highest broadband speed belongs to Singapore with an average speed of 245.5Mbits/s.

The highest and lowest average broadband price in AUD for unlimited data at 60Mbytes/s or more for each country in 2022 is shown above. A line at $10 per month and $100 per month is used to easily see prices in countries that are above or below the thresholds.

United Arab Emirates has the most expensive broadband at $153.88 AUD while Ukraine has the cheapest at $6.93 AUD. Australia is ranked 16th in the world at $76.52 AUD and is highlighted in red.

Australian subscribers by connection types from 2013 to 2018

Total data downloads in Australia from 2013 to 2018

From the chart above, the number of subscribers for different connection types from 2013 to 2018 bi-annually in Australia can be compared. The chart can be filtered to show a specific broadband type and allow trends to be seen.

Digital subscriber line or DSL subscribers in Australia has slowly declined while fibre users has increased exponentially throughout the years. The number of Cable and Mobile connection users have stayed relatively constant throughout the years.

The line chart above shows the trend of data downloads in Australia from 2013 to 2018 in Terabytes. The increase in data download is related to the increasing internet users as well as the improvement in both mobile and broadband speeds, allowing users to download more data faster.

Mobile data downloads has grown about 12.57 times from 19,636TB to 246,765TB while broadband data downloads increased about 5.83 times from 657,262TB to 3,837,215TB.

Internet freedom score 2021

Internet freedom of countries in 2021

The chart above shows the highest and lowest total scoring countries from an analysis of internet freedom around the world from Freedom on the Net survey in 2021. The three main categories assessed are Obstacles to Access, Limits on Content and Violations of User Rights scored out of 25, 35 and 40 respectively.

The three categories are summed together to calculate the total internet freedom score. Australia has a total score of 75 with the highest with Iceland at 96 and lowest with China at 10.

Following the internet freedom score from the previous chart, the 70 countries analysed are categorised into either Not free from total score of 0 to 39, Partly free between 40 to 69 and Free for score of 70 to 100.

Australia with a score of 75 is a part of 21 countries out of the 70 countries analysed or 30% that are classified as Free. The majority of the countries at 31 or 44.29% is considered Partly Free while only 18 countries or 25.71% are Not Free.
